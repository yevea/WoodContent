    6. Cracks, Knots and Deformations

      yevea.com/#defectos-deformaciones

      Most people today cannot distinguish between artificial wood (chipboard + veneer) and solid wood, which causes unjustified expectations of wood perfection. Olive wood is full of knots and cracks that form part of its nature — see olive wood imperfections. Wood is a living element subject to changes in ambient temperature and humidity, so it will tend to expand and contract to adapt to its environment. This movement of the wood can produce cracks or deformations over time that do not alter its functionality or natural beauty. Every customer, before purchasing a board or worktop from us, must be aware that olive wood is profoundly imperfect. The uniqueness of olive wood lies in its imperfections and its spectacular grain. The spectacular grain (heterogeneity) excludes perfection in the sense of industrial materials (homogeneity). It is not possible to have wood with spectacular grain and perfect wood without cracks at the same time. These two parameters are mutually exclusive. There is no wood with grain that has no cracks, especially at Yevea where we specialise in producing wood with lots of grain, cracks and knots. In some products, we fill the cracks with resin. Claims for uncovered or covered cracks and knots will not be accepted.

    7. Colour and Grain of Olive Wood

      yevea.com/info/es/condiciones.html#defectos-color

      We manufacture solid olive wood from olive trees originating from olive grove renovations, where there is usually a mix of different varieties with their respective differences in wood colour. The varieties enter the sawmill mixed, and as they come out, we dry the planks. During drying, all the wood turns dark grey. When we manufacture the board, it is impossible to select olive strips that will have the same grain and colour. The possible variations in colour and grain of olive wood are innumerable. Claims regarding the colour or grain of the wood, whether by excess or defect, will not be accepted, as this aspect is subjective.

    8. Fissures in Boards

      yevea.com/info/es/condiciones.html#defectos-fisuras

      Fissures (in the joints) are quite common in solid wood boards and worktops, especially those made of olive wood. Their cause is the movement or contraction of the wood strips due to changes in ambient humidity. Changes in humidity move the strips because natural wood is a hygroscopic material. To limit this movement, the board must be kept well saturated with maintenance oil. If, despite this, the strips have moved, we will send the customer a repair kit with which the joint can be easily repaired. Returns or exchanges due to fissures in the boards resulting from lack of maintenance (wood oil treatment) will not be accepted.

    9. Warping and Bowing

      yevea.com/info/es/condiciones.html#defectos-curvamiento

      Warping and bowing occur frequently due to the inherent nature of olive wood. To prevent warping and bowing, the customer must screw the board to the support structure immediately upon receiving it, before the wood can move. Returns or exchanges due to defects in the boards resulting from faulty storage, installation or assembly will not be accepted. For example, if a solid wood board (not fibreboard) is not screwed to a rigid support, it will inevitably warp and bow. This is the natural response of wood to differences in humidity between the upper side and the underside of the board. This is something any professional knows about natural wood boards.
