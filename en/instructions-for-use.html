Instructions for Use and Maintenance of Olive Wood

To keep the warranty valid, the present olive wood maintenance instructions must be followed.

  1. Outdoor Use

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#exterior

    We do not provide a warranty for outdoor use. NOTE: In practice, many customers have our boards outdoors without any problems as long as the treatment is appropriate. In this case, the oil finish must be replaced with a lacquer specifically designed for outdoor use.

  2. Sunlight

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#sol

    Wooden boards exposed to sunlight will change their wood tone. If some parts of the board receive more sunlight than others, the sunny areas and the "shadows" (e.g. from a small tablecloth) will be visible on the board. When purchasing the board, it is possible to request a treatment with sun protection.

  3. Water

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#agua

    Avoid leaving the board treated with natural oil in contact with water for a prolonged period, e.g. with wet cloths, cups, glasses, etc. If the board gets flooded with liquids, it should be dried with a cloth immediately. If a whitish ring appears, apply a little treatment oil to the area and the wood will recover its natural tone. As the board becomes more saturated with oil, it will become much harder for stains or rings to form. If, for any reason, the customer wishes a completely water-resistant board, they should request a lacquer treatment.

  4. Hobs and Sinks

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#hornillas-fregaderos

    If the board is located near a hob, sink, or above a dishwasher, washing machine, or any other heat source, it will likely dry out faster and require more frequent maintenance. For a board exposed in this way, it is advisable to apply oil when the wood appears to be turning lighter.

  5. Flower Pots

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#macetas

    Flower pots should not be placed directly on wooden boards, even if there is a clay bowl or plate underneath.

  6. Cutting Boards

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#tablas-cortar

    Although Yevea boards are cutting boards in their own right, we recommend not cutting directly on the board, but rather using a separate cutting board.

  7. Breathing

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#respiracion

    Solid wood boards cannot be mounted on an airtight support, embedded inside a wall, or covered in such a way that air cannot reach the wood from both the top and bottom of the board. There must always be at least a gap for air access. The wood needs to be able to "breathe".

  8. Storage

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#almacenamiento

    In general, any solid wood, and especially olive wood due to its irregular grain and the strength of its fibres, will warp if it is not screwed to a stable base. If you need to store the boards before their final installation, you should take some considerations into account.

    1. Remove the boards from their original packaging and check whether they have suffered any damage during transport.
    2. Remember that the company only accepts claims for damage suffered during transport within 24 hours of receipt.
    3. The boards cannot be stored with one face exposed to air and the other sealed, such as placing them on a flat surface (floor).
    4. The best way to store the boards is horizontally, but with battens underneath to ensure airflow to both sides of the board, and with some weight on top.
    5. If you have several boards, it is advisable to place battens between them and secure the boards together with straps.
    6. Ideally, the boards should be placed in their final position on the same day they are received.
    7. Keep them away from direct sunlight.
    8. Keep them away from moisture.

  9. Cracks

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#grietas

    Over time, due to changes in temperature and humidity inside our homes and the natural movement of the wood as it adapts to these changes, some small cracks may appear on the surface of the olive wood board. These should not be a cause for concern — they are micro-cracks that will only add beauty to our boards. Cracks, like knots, are not defects but inherent properties of natural wood. Regular maintenance of the wood with oil is very important to keep the wood well hydrated and flexible to movement. Medium cracks can be easily repaired by filling them with wax. If your board develops large cracks, please contact us to resolve the issue.

  10. Humidity

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#humedad

    The wooden board adapts to the humidity levels of its environment. The optimal conditions for wood are approximately 50% humidity, which is the normal humidity level in homes where the temperature is maintained at around 20°C. Humidity is important with respect to the expansion or contraction of the board. The board absorbs or releases moisture in sync with changes in ambient humidity. If humidity is high, the board may change its size by up to 1 cm.

  11. Cleaning

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#limpieza

    The wooden board can be cleaned regularly with a cloth and a soapy solution. It does not require any special care in its cleaning.

  12. Sanding

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#lijado

    Scratches can be completely repaired and removed with a light sanding, although olive wood is a very hard wood and it is not easy to cause damage or scratches on it. Use 180-grit sandpaper. Never use a scouring pad or coarse-grit sandpaper. The board can be partially sanded, only in the affected areas. Keep in mind that sanding removes the protective layers of the wood, and the sanded surface must be treated again with Yevea Timberoil maintenance oil.

  13. Oil Treatment

    yevea.com/info/en/instructions-for-use.html#madera-aceite

    The boards leave the factory finished by default with our 100% natural Yevea treatment oil. To keep the board always hydrated and perfectly waterproofed, we recommend using our Yevea maintenance oil. Its application is very easy: simply put a little oil in a spray bottle and clean the board with this product once a week. The board will always remain hydrated, waterproofed, and with radiant and luminous olive tones.

    1. Clean the board with a mild detergent.
    2. Dry the board.
    3. Shake the oil container well.
    4. Spray a small amount of oil evenly over the surface.
    5. Spread the oil over the entire surface of the board with a paper towel or soft cloth.
    6. Wipe again with a clean towel or cloth to remove any residue.

    Since the oil contains a waterproofing base, if drops are left or too much oil is applied, marks, "puddles" or small solidified oil drops will remain. Therefore, it is advisable to apply a very small amount each time, well spread out.
